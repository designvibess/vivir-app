## 1. Always use (Spain standard)

- **vosotros** for informal plural (not ustedes — ustedes is formal or Latin American)
- **tú** for informal singular (not vos — voseo is not used in Spain)
- **present perfect for recent past**: "He comido" (not "Comí") for "I ate today"
- **peninsular /θ/ distinction**: z and c+e/i = /θ/ (like "th" in "think"). This is standard Spain Spanish; seseo (using /s/) is regional (parts of Andalucía, Canarias) but not standard.

## 4. Verb forms to check

- **Vosotros present**: -áis/-éis/-ís (habláis, coméis, vivís)
- **Vosotros preterite**: -asteis/-isteis (hablasteis, comisteis)
- **Vosotros imperfect**: -abais/-íais (hablabais, comíais)
- **Vosotros future**: -éis (hablaréis, comeréis)
- **Vosotros imperative**: drop final -d of infinitive (¡hablad!, ¡comed!, ¡vivid!)
- **Vosotros subjunctive**: -éis/-áis (habléis, comáis)

## 5. Address patterns

- **Tú**: default for most situations in Spain (shops, restaurants, neighbours, colleagues of similar age)
- **Usted**: reserved for elderly people, formal official contexts, some older-generation service workers
- **Vosotros**: any informal group; friends, family, people at a table
- **Ustedes**: formal groups, or addressing people you'd each individually address as usted

## 6. Phonetic features (for audio notes)

- **Distinción (standard)**: z and c+e/i = /θ/. Examples: gracias → "gra-THYASS", coche → "KO-cheh", cerveza → "ther-VEH-thah"
- **Seseo**: Some Andalusian and Canarian speakers use /s/ for z/c+e/i. Note in lessons as regional, not standard.
- **Lleísmo vs yeísmo**: Most Spain speakers use yeísmo (ll = y sound). Distinción (ll ≠ y) is regional.
- **Aspiration of final -s**: Heard in southern Spain and some regions. Don't teach as standard.
